# Profile Comparison Reflection

- High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi: Pop pushed upbeat tracks like Sunrise City and Gym Hero, while Chill Lofi promoted low-energy tracks like Library Rain and Midnight Coding. This makes sense because the energy target changed from 0.90 to 0.38 and the genre anchor switched from pop to lofi.
- High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock: Both profiles favored high energy, but genre and mood split the top song. Deep Intense Rock correctly put Storm Runner first due to rock+intense+high-energy alignment.
- Chill Lofi vs Deep Intense Rock: These profiles produced almost opposite styles. Chill Lofi emphasized calm acoustic-leaning songs, while Deep Intense Rock pulled intense songs with very high energy.
- Conflicting Chill + Very High Energy vs Chill Lofi: The conflicting profile still ranked Spacewalk Thoughts first mostly due to genre+mood points, even though its energy was far from target. This reveals how categorical features can overpower numerical mismatch.
- Genreless Energy-Only vs High-Energy Pop: With no genre or mood preference, rankings became almost pure energy matching. That shows why explicit categorical preferences improve recommendation specificity.
